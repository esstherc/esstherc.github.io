Here's a little about how I work.

Across research labs, tech startups, and complex spatial systems, I’ve led UX research and product design to turn abstract geospatial algorithms and clinical workflows into accessible tools.

My purpose in design is to create order out of chaos. I crave complexity and love to tame it into intuitive interfaces.
I dive into real, messy user journeys rather than relying on perfect personas. I use design research to strip down assumptions and define a true Information Architecture: uncovering what we should build, and why.
I visualize complex ecosystems. As a data viz enthusiast, I turn abstract data into clear visual hierarchies to help engineering and product stakeholders build a common understanding.
You might say I’m a designer with a PM bent; I spend lots of time listening and facilitating to balance user needs, dev constraints, and project goals, ensuring we design beautifully while actually driving retention and conversion.
